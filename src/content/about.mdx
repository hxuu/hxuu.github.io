Having spent almost **3 years** pentesting and doing security research, I was obsessed with vulnerabilities that require actually understanding the spec before you can reliably exploit it. I got here through CTFs, and a research habit that hasn't let up *(currently studying browser extension security btw).*

I currently am completing my undergraduate in cybersecurity. To that end, I work as a bug bounty hunter —— [CVE-2026-4006](https://www.cve.org/CVERecord?id=CVE-2026-4006) is my recent finding. I am also pretty heavily involved with scientific clubs, where I author **capture-the-flag** security challenges and organize competitions for [IngeHack CTF](https://github.com/Ingeniums/IngeHack-2k26-Challenges) and the [Algerian Tech Makers](https://github.com/hxuu/bunianCTF). Other than that, I’ve dabbled in DevOps/infrastructure with some friends at ESI-SBA. If you want to see more of what I do, check out my [/work](https://vhxuu.xyz/work) page as well as my [/blog](https://vhxuu.xyz/blog).

Outside of my professional life, I enjoy everything Linux: vim, tmux, dwm, and every tool that increases speed.
